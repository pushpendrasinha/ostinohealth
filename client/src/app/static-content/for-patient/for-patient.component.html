You are not alone. Majority of patients do not take their medications as prescribed; some do not take it at all. It is a huge problem. The practice has become commonplace; to the extent that various medical associations in India are nowadays alluding to it as an epidemic.

We did our research and listed below are some of the reasons cited for non-compliance.

  • Symptoms have disappeared , there is no rationale for continuing taking pills. If symptoms reappear, the game changes and it is back to the schedule.
  • Affordability issue, unable to avail transportation to a pharmacy store. You have to make decisions especially in one of the world's most affordable markets kept in place by Government regulations.
  • Elderly patients put on multiple medicines each with its own timetable would need full time secretarial assistance to organize. Understand the issue.
  • Some have parents / grandparents at home dependent on pills and hence promise never to become like them. In fact, after interacting with them, we do not blame them.
  • I do not have regular eating routines and hence unable to remember taking medicines. Common and yet counter-intuitive; building right habits is the key to maintaining compliance.
  • Medical science is still in its infancy and there are other ways to get better such as Ayurveda, Unani, Alternative Therapies; the list goes on. Possible not probable.
  • Psychology effect; linking taking medicines to failure. We do not understand this aspect.
  • Age denial syndrome; not ready to accept that they are aging.
  • Treatment interfering with work responsibilities that could compromise their ability to continue working. Cannot be denied.
  • Mistrust of the medical profession basis Google news and media stories on malpractice. Fear of adverse effects much more than the good effects.
  • Losing hope that treatment can solve disease and not be symptomatic in nature.
  • Faith moves mountains. I would put myself in God's hands as opposed to man made chemicals.
We thought through in depth, interacted with Medical Professionals and patients, mapped out issues, developed hypothesis, ran pilots and arrived at the conclusion that, simply put, it is the absence of right habits and consistent routines that cause non-adherence.

Listed below are Steps 1, 2 and 3 that we recommend:
1. Store drugs near the place where it is being used. Practical solution, yet we need to ensure caution, such as presence of kids at home.
2. Externalize the reminders through calendar entries or through technology such as mobile apps designed to do just this.
3. Club it with an old habit. Our research threw up linking mechanisms of high adherence patients; mental linking of taking pills to specific activities such as evening TV series.
